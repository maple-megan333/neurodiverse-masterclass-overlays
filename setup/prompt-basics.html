🧱 The 4-Element Framework

Every great prompt has four parts. You don't always need all four, but knowing them means you can troubleshoot.

R

Role

Tell AI who to be. "You are a patient writing coach" gets different results than "You are a strict editor."

C

Context

Give background. Who it's for, what you've tried, what the situation is.

T

Task

Be specific. "Help me write" is vague. "Write a 3-paragraph email declining a meeting politely" is specific.

F

Format

Tell AI how to structure output. Bullet points? Table? Word count? Say so.

🧪 Practice: Build Three Prompts

✏️
Exercise 1 — The Email:
Exercise 1
Role: You are a professional communication coach.
Context: I need to email my manager about pushing back a deadline. The project is delayed because I'm waiting on another team's input.
Task: Write a polite but firm email explaining the delay and proposing a new timeline.
Format: Under 150 words. Professional but warm.
✏️
Exercise 2 — The Explanation:
Exercise 2
Role: You are a teacher great at making complex things simple.
Context: I'm trying to understand how mortgage interest rates work. No finance background.
Task: Explain mortgage rates using a real-world analogy.
Format: 3 short paragraphs, no jargon, include one example with numbers.
✏️
Exercise 3 — The Decision:
Exercise 3
Role: You are a decision-making coach.
Context: I'm deciding whether to take a new job — 20% more pay but longer commute and less flexibility.
Task: Ask me 5 questions to help me figure out what matters most, then create a pros/cons framework.
Format: Numbered questions, then a table after I answer.

Just remember: "Be specific about what you want, and tell AI who to be." That gets you 70% there.
