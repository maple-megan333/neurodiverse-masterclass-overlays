What RAG is, in plain language

Every AI tool you've used was trained on a snapshot of the internet. It knows nothing about your meeting notes, your Notion pages, your bookmarked PDFs, your medical records.

RAG — Retrieval-Augmented Generation — fixes that. The AI searches your documents first, pulls the relevant passages, then answers from those. Same conversation interface, grounded in your actual life.

The shorthand: "AI that answers from a folder you point it at."

The 3-layer model

Almost every RAG question collapses to: "How much building am I willing to do?" Three layers, each a real ceiling. Pick the lowest one that solves your problem.

Layer 1 · no-code

Drop docs into a chat tool

Upload files into a Claude Project, NotebookLM, ChatGPT Project, Gemini Gem, or Perplexity Space. Tool handles everything else.

  • Setup time: 5 minutes
  • Cost: $0–$25/mo
  • Fits: ~85% of personal use
Layer 2 · low-code

Personal knowledge base

Obsidian + Smart Connections, AnythingLLM + Ollama, Notion AI Q&A, or Mem.ai. You manage a vault; the tool indexes it.

  • Setup time: 1–2 hours
  • Cost: $0–$15/mo
  • Fits: 200+ docs, existing vault, privacy
Layer 3 · production

Build your own pipeline

Vector database (Chroma, Qdrant, pgvector, Pinecone) + embedding model + framework (LlamaIndex, LangChain). Code required.

  • Setup time: days to weeks
  • Cost: $10–$2,000+/mo
  • Fits: building for other people
⚠️
Most people who think they need Layer 3 actually need Layer 1 or 2

Layer 3 is what tutorials, agency pitch decks, and breathless YouTube videos love to talk about. It's the most expensive option, the slowest to set up, and — for personal knowledge — almost always overkill.

Layer 3 is correct when you're shipping a product that has to retrieve from your data for other people. If you're managing your own notes, files, or research, Layer 1 or 2 will outperform a homemade pipeline on cost, reliability, and time-to-first-useful-answer.

Permission to start at Layer 1. You're not behind. You're being efficient.

Privacy is the foundation

🛡️
When local-only is non-negotiable

Some content shouldn't touch a cloud provider's servers, full stop. The four big categories:

  • Medical — diagnoses, lab results, therapy notes, prescription histories
  • Legal — case files, contracts under negotiation, discovery materials, anything privileged
  • Therapy / mental health — your own journal, processing notes, crisis-period writing
  • Financial — tax records, investment specifics, anything with an account number

For any of those, choose a tool from the local-only column:

  • Obsidian + Smart Connections — on-device BGE-micro embeddings, no API key, no network call
  • AnythingLLM + Ollama — desktop app, fully air-gappable, Rust-based audio transcription stays local
  • Self-hosted Qdrant + local embedding model — Layer 3 option for technical users

If a tool isn't on this list, assume the documents you upload may be used to train future models unless the vendor explicitly says otherwise in writing.
