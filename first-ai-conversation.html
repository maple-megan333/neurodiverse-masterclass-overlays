Step 1: Open Claude and Say Hello

Research on automation trust calibration shows that people who understand a system's limitations use it more effectively (Lee & See, 2004).

ð¡What good looks like

Claude explains how it generates responses. Key insight: AI sounds confident whether right or wrong. The tone tells you nothing about accuracy.

âDo this now
  1. Open claude.ai
  2. Copy and paste this prompt into a new conversation:
Hi! I'm trying AI for the first time. I want to understand what you can and can't do. Can you explain in 3-4 casual sentences: what are you actually doing when I ask you a question? Keep it non-technical.
  1. Read the response. Notice how it sounds confident and clear.
  2. Reflect: Did Claude sound like it really knew? Did it explain itself in a way that made sense?

Step 2: Test the Confidence Thing

âCopy paste this 
Give me 3 passionate reasons why pineapple belongs on pizza. Then 3 reasons why it doesn't. After both, tell me: were you 'believing' either side?

AI argued both sides with equal confidence. Confidence and accuracy are completely separate. This is huge. AI will argue any position with the same tone. You have to be the judge.

Step 3: Have a Real Conversation

âThink of something you need help with

Fill in the brackets and paste this prompt:

I need help with [YOUR TASK]. Before you help me: what's one thing you'd want to know? Ask me that one question first.

Examples to fill in:

  • "I need help with organizing my files"
  • "I need help with writing a tough email"
  • "I need help with planning a project"
  • "I need help with understanding a topic I'm confused about"
ð¡What good looks like

The response addresses YOUR situation, not generic advice. Claude asked you a question first instead of lecturing. That's how conversations work. The best AI interactions are back-and-forths, not one-way answers.
