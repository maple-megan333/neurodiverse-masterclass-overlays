💡 Anti-Anchoring Brainstorms
📘
What you'll build
Generate genuinely novel ideas.
✏️
Try this when you're ready
Paste into Claude: 
I'm brainstorming about [CHALLENGE]. Give me 10 completely different approaches. After #10, add 3 more combining your most unusual ideas.
⭐
What good looks like
The 10 approaches come from at least 4 different angles — not 10 variations of the same idea. At least 2 make you say "huh, weird." The 3 combo ideas are stranger than the originals. None mention "leverage," "synergy," or "innovative solution."
☁️
Low Energy
"Give me 5 weird ideas for [thing]." Pick the one that makes you laugh.
🍝 Creative Briefs
📘
What you'll build
Vague impulse → clear project brief.
✏️
Try this when you're ready
Paste into Claude: 
I have a creative idea: [DESCRIBE]. Help me turn this into a brief by asking 5 questions about audience, tone, format, constraints, and success. Then write a 1-page brief.
⭐
What good looks like
The 5 questions surface things you hadn't thought about — usually around audience or success criteria. The brief reads back your idea sharper than you described it. A collaborator could pick it up cold and know what to make. "Success" is measurable, not aspirational.
☁️
Low Energy
"Turn this vague idea into one clear sentence: [idea]"
🚀 First Draft Momentum
📘
What you'll build
Get past the blank page fast.
✏️
Try this when you're ready
Paste into Claude: 
I need to write [WHAT]. Don't write it for me. Give me:
1. 5 possible opening lines
2. 3 structural approaches
3. One question that will unlock the rest

Help me START, not finish.
⭐
What good looks like
At least one opening line surprises you — feels nothing like an AI hook. The 3 structures are genuinely different shapes (chronological, argumentative, vignette-led — not all variations on "intro/body/conclusion"). The unlock question is the kind you'd ask a friend at coffee, not a corporate prompt.
☁️
Low Energy
"Give me the first sentence of [thing]. Make it surprising."
🔄 Feedback Translation
📘
What you'll build
Vague feedback → actionable direction.
✏️
Try this when you're ready
Paste into Claude: 
I got this feedback on my [PROJECT]: [PASTE]

Translate into specific changes. For each:
1. What they mean
2. One thing I can do
3. Priority (high/medium/low)
⭐
What good looks like
Vague comments like "make it pop" get translated into concrete moves ("increase contrast on the hero image"). Each "one thing I can do" is a single action under 30 minutes. Priorities are honest — not everything ranked high. You walk away knowing the first change to make.
☁️
Low Energy
Paste feedback → "What's the one thing to change first?"
