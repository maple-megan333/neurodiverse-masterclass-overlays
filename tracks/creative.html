Your Creative Catalyst

AI Creative Projects

Unlock your creative potential by using AI as a brainstorming partner, accountability coach, and creative co-creator.

Brainstorming Partner ▼

Use AI as your thinking partner to generate ideas, explore possibilities, and break creative blocks. The goal is expanding your thinking, not replacing it.

Divergent Idea Generation: Ask for many ideas with different angles.

I'm working on [project]. The core idea is [brief description]. Can you brainstorm 10 different directions for this? I'm looking for ideas that are: - [Constraint 1: e.g., unexpected] - [Constraint 2: e.g., grounded in my audience's real problems] Just list options. I'll pick what resonates.

Angle Finder: Discover new perspectives on a familiar topic.

I'm creating content about [topic]. I already have these angles covered: - [Angle 1] - [Angle 2] What other angles or perspectives could I explore? What would my audience want to know that I haven't thought about?

Constraint-Based Creativity: Use limitations to spark innovation.

I want to create [type of work] with these constraints: - [Constraint 1: e.g., must use only existing assets] - [Constraint 2: e.g., maximum 500 words] - [Constraint 3: e.g., must appeal to beginners] What are some unexpected approaches that work within these limits?
Creative Brief Builder ▼

Turn a vague creative vision into a clear brief that guides execution. Use AI to structure your thinking and identify what's actually important.

Brief from Rough Notes: Organize scattered ideas into a proper brief.

I'm creating [project type]. Here are my raw thoughts: [Paste your scattered notes, feelings, reference images, ideas] Can you help me organize this into a clear creative brief? Structure it with: - Core concept - Target audience - Key message - Style/tone - Reference inspiration - Success metrics

Audience Deep Dive: Understand who you're creating for.

I'm creating [project] for [target audience description]. Help me go deeper: - What are their biggest frustrations? - What do they already believe about this? - What would actually move them? - What language/references would resonate? - What would make them trust this?

Success Definition: Get clear on what "done" looks like.

My creative project: [Description] How will I know if this is successful? Help me define: - The emotional response I want - The action I want people to take - The metrics that matter - What "good enough" looks like - When I should stop iterating
First Draft Momentum ▼

Break through perfectionism and get your first draft done. AI can help you start, keep momentum, and resist the urge to over-edit prematurely.

Start the Draft: Get words on the page, even if rough.

I need to write [what you're creating]. Here's what I want to say: [Your messy thoughts about the topic] I'm getting stuck on the opening. Can you write me a strong first few lines that: - [Hook them with] - [Introduce the topic as] - [Avoid] Then I'll take it from there.

Fill the Middle: Keep momentum through the hard part.

Here's what I have so far: [Paste your draft so far] I'm getting lost in the middle. My next section should cover [topic]. What are the key points I need to hit? Give me a bullet outline and I'll expand it.

Momentum Over Perfection: Finish first, refine later.

I have this draft: [Paste your work-in-progress] I'm not happy with [specific part], but I want to finish the draft first. Should I push through or does this section need fixing before I continue? Give me permission to be imperfect here.
Feedback Translation ▼

Process feedback without losing your creative vision. Use AI to understand what's actually being said and decide what to implement.

Decode Vague Feedback: Understand what someone really means.

I got this feedback on my work: "[Feedback]" What do you think they actually mean? What's the core criticism here? Is it about [specific element]? What should I actually change?

Conflicting Feedback Resolution: When reviewers disagree.

My work [description] got conflicting feedback: - Person A said: "[Feedback 1]" - Person B said: "[Feedback 2]" These feel opposite. What's the real issue here? Is there a solution that addresses both concerns? Or should I lean toward one perspective?

Maintain Your Vision: Know when to ignore feedback.

I got feedback to [change X], but I feel strongly about keeping it because [your reason]. Is this ego, or am I protecting something important? Help me evaluate: should I defend this choice or let it go?
Creative Sprint Planning ▼

Structure focused creative work into productive sessions. Use AI to plan sprints that maximize flow and output.

Sprint Goal Setting: Define what you'll accomplish in a time block.

I'm planning a creative sprint: [Duration - e.g., 4 hours tomorrow morning] My project: [Description] Current state: [What I have] The sprint should accomplish: [What you want to finish] Help me break this into phases: 1. [Setup/warming up] 2. [Main work] 3. [Iteration] 4. [Wrap up] What's realistic for this timeframe?

Environmental Design: Plan conditions for creative flow.

I'm doing a creative sprint on [date/time]. I want to optimize my environment. My context: - Where I'm working: [Location] - How long: [Duration] - Type of work: [Description] - What usually distracts me: [List] What should I prep? What should I remove? What's my ideal setup?

Recovery & Reflection: Capture insights from your sprint.

I just finished a creative sprint. Here's what happened: - What I planned: [Original goal] - What I actually did: [What happened] - How I felt: [Your experience] - Where I got stuck: [Blockers] - What surprised me: [Discoveries] What should I learn from this for next time?
