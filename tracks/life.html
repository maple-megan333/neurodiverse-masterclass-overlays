Your Life Operating System

AI for Life Management

Use AI to organize your life, reduce cognitive load, and make space for what actually matters to you.

Morning Brain Dump ▼

Start your day with clarity. Use AI to capture everything on your mind and organize it into actionable priorities.

Brain to Priorities: Convert scattered thoughts into a clean action list.

Here's everything on my mind this morning: [Dump all your thoughts, worries, tasks, ideas - raw and unfiltered] Can you help me organize this? I need: 1. What's actually urgent today 2. What can wait until later this week 3. What I'm overthinking (reassure me) 4. What I should just let go of 5. My top 3 priorities for today

Anxiety Processing: Process worry productively.

I'm feeling anxious about: [What's worrying you] Help me untangle this. Is this: - Something I can control? (What's my action?) - Something I can partially influence? (What's my 20%?) - Something I need to accept? (How do I let it go?) What's the productive move here?

Decision Inbox: Collect decisions to make intentionally.

I have these decisions to make: 1. [Decision 1] 2. [Decision 2] 3. [Decision 3] Which are urgent? Which can I batch? For each one, what information do I actually need before deciding?
Calendar & Routines ▼

Structure your time to protect what matters. Use AI to design routines and catch scheduling problems early.

Build a Protective Routine: Design time for what actually matters to you.

My typical week: [Describe your commitments and free time patterns] What matters most to me: - [Priority 1] - [Priority 2] - [Priority 3] Am I protecting time for these? What routine would actually work with my life? Help me design a realistic schedule.

Time Audit: Understand where your time actually goes.

My week looks like: - Work: [Hours/description] - Family: [Hours/description] - Exercise: [Hours/description] - Admin tasks: [Hours/description] - Free time: [Hours/description] Where's my time actually going? What's consuming energy? What could I reclaim?

Schedule Optimization: Catch problems before they happen.

Here's my calendar for [week/month]: [Describe major commitments and timing] Does this look sustainable? What conflicts do you see coming? When will I be most overwhelmed? How should I adjust?
Decision Fatigue Reducer ▼

Make big decisions with confidence and eliminate decision fatigue on the small stuff. Use AI for thinking and frameworks, not answers.

Big Decision Framework: Structure major life decisions.

I'm facing this decision: [What you're deciding] Context: - Why I'm deciding now: [What triggered this] - What's at stake: [What matters here] - My constraints: [What I can't change] - My values: [What guides me] Help me think through this. What frameworks would help? What am I not considering?

Option Analysis: Evaluate choices systematically.

I'm choosing between: - Option A: [Description] - Option B: [Description] - Option C: [Description] For each option, help me evaluate: - What's the best case scenario? - What's the realistic case? - What could go wrong? - Can I change course if needed? - Does it align with my values?

Decision Automation: Remove tiny decisions entirely.

I'm decision-fatigued by: - [Small decision 1] - [Small decision 2] - [Small decision 3] Help me design systems or rules so I don't have to decide these anymore. What's a "good enough" default?
Learning Plans ▼

Learn deliberately. Use AI to design learning paths, break down complex topics, and adjust as you grow.

Learning Roadmap: Create a realistic path from beginner to competent.

I want to learn: [Topic/skill] My context: - Why: [Your motivation] - Timeline: [When do you need this] - Current level: [Where you're starting] - How much time/week: [Your realistic capacity] - Learning style: [How you learn best] Build me a learning roadmap. What should I learn first? What's a realistic progression? What's the fastest path?

Topic Deep Dive: Understand something complex at your level.

I'm trying to understand [complex topic], but [where you're stuck]. Explain this to me like I'm [your actual background]. What's the core concept? What can I ignore? What's the absolute minimum I need to know?

Practice Plan: Design learning with application.

I'm learning [skill]. So far I've [what you've done]. I want to practice by [your project idea]. Is this a good practice project? What specific things should I try? What mistakes will I probably make (so I can watch for them)?
Life Admin Batch ▼

Handle all your life admin tasks efficiently by batching them. Use AI to organize, prioritize, and create systems that stick.

Admin Backlog Triage: Get annoying stuff organized and planned.

Here's my life admin backlog (all the stuff I've been avoiding): - [Task 1] - [Task 2] - [Task 3] [... etc] Help me organize this: 1. What's actually urgent? 2. What can I batch together? 3. What can I delegate or eliminate? 4. What's realistic to do this weekend?

System Building: Create processes so you don't have to think about stuff.

I keep getting stuck on: [Recurring admin task] This happens because: [Why it's hard] I want it to be: [Your desired outcome] Help me design a system for this. What would make it automatic or at least mindless? Where should I do it? When? With what tools?

Batching Strategy: Cluster similar tasks to minimize friction.

I have these admin tasks to do: - [Task 1] - [Task 2] - [Task 3] - [Task 4] - [Task 5] How should I batch these? What's the most efficient order? What should I prep beforehand? Can I do any of these while doing something else?
