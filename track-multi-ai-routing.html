The Triple Stack workflow

The dominant pattern in 2026 is a three-stage chain: gather facts with citations, synthesize with a long-context model, shape the output. The trick is the handoff — pasting clean context between tools so you keep momentum and skip re-explaining yourself.

📝
Example: a competitor brief for a Monday meeting
  1. Perplexity ProAsk: "What did [Competitor X] ship in the last 90 days? Include source links." Copy the citation list.
  2. Claude Sonnet 4.6Open a Project. Paste the Perplexity output plus past meeting notes. Ask: "Synthesize into a one-page brief in my voice with the three biggest roadmap implications." 1M context handles it in one shot.
  3. ChatGPT (GPT-5.5)Paste the Claude brief. Ask: "Turn this into a 5-slide outline with one image prompt per slide." Hand the prompts to GPT Image 2 or Midjourney V8.

Total elapsed: ~25 minutes. Each tool spent its strength on the slice it does best.

🔗
Context-passing tactic
Paste with a one-line header: --- From Perplexity, 2026-05-05 ---. Labeled context is treated more reliably than raw paste, and you can scroll back later to find where a claim came from.

Voice routing for low-energy days

The hardest moments aren't the hard tasks — they're the days when typing itself is the wall. Voice mode is the workaround: you keep moving, the AI types. Two strong options as of mid-2026.

ChatGPT Advanced Voice

Plus $20/mo

Real-time, interruptible voice with camera vision. Best for dictating a draft or asking it to "look at this" on your phone screen.

  • Live, interruptible mid-sentence
  • Camera input lets it see what you're working on
  • Memory persists across sessions (review settings)
  • Best when you want real-time reactivity

Claude Voice (beta)

Free on all plans

Voice layer over Claude's longer reasoning. Continues a typed chat by voice or talks through a complex document already in context.

  • Free on every plan, including no-cost accounts
  • Picks up existing chats — no re-explaining context
  • Better at long-form thinking out loud
  • Best when the task needs depth, not banter
🎧
Lower-spoon option
If voice still feels like work, hand sources to NotebookLM and request an Audio Overview — a 12-minute conversational podcast instead of a 40-page PDF. That's matching the medium to your capacity, not procrastination.

Long context = no prep tax

Before 1M-token context windows, working with a long document meant a chore-list before the real work: split the PDF, summarize chapters, paste the chunks. For ND brains, that prep often killed the task — 30-45 minutes of executive function spent before any thinking began.

Gemini 2.5 Pro and Claude Opus 4.7 both handle 1M tokens. Gemini holds 99.7% recall across the full window. Practical implication: load the whole research corpus, contract, or board pack in one paste and ask your real question first.

🎯
The ND-specific win
The hidden cost of "summarize then think" workflows isn't time — it's the activation energy of the summarization step. Long context removes it. The task you'd avoided for two weeks becomes a 90-second paste. That shift — from "I need a clear afternoon" to "I can do this now" — is the single biggest unlock in the 2026 tool stack.

Decision fatigue mitigation

Multi-AI mastery without guardrails can flip into the opposite problem: ten open tabs, three half-finished prompts, and no completed work. The research is clear on the cap.

  • Cap daily tool switching at three. The Triple Stack covers ~90% of professional workflows. Past three tools per day, interface-switching overhead compounds and marginal value drops fast.
  • Goblin Tools (free) before a big AI session. Magic ToDo breaks a vague intent into ND-readable subtasks. 60 seconds there and the prompt you arrive with is dramatically cleaner.
  • Automation as cognitive load reduction. An n8n or Zapier workflow that auto-routes "inbox → Notion" or "research alert → Perplexity → Claude" eliminates the recurring "when do I do this?" decision. One afternoon of setup, months of unmade decisions.
  • Pre-decide your defaults. Write down "When I need X, I open Y." Tape it to your monitor. Skip the meta-decision on days when picking the tool is the hardest part.

Putting it all together — a sample week

One week of routing the Triple Stack against energy state. Capacity rarely runs flat across five days; the tool stack can track that.

Monday · High energy

Triple Stack on a new initiative. Build the n8n workflow you've been putting off. Spend the setup energy that compounds later in the week.

Tuesday · Medium energy

Email and short-form in ChatGPT. One Cursor 3 session for small code tasks. Skip new tool exploration today.

Wednesday · Low energy

Claude Voice for the document you've been avoiding. NotebookLM Audio Overview during a walk for unread research. Output is real even when typing isn't.

Thursday · Medium energy

Gemini 2.5 Pro on the long PDF you couldn't face Monday — ask the real question first, no chunking. One ChatGPT pass for output polish.

Friday · High or low (swap accordingly)

Triple Stack on the week's biggest deliverable. Or, if Friday is your crash day, Claude Voice plus a 10-minute "next week's defaults" pre-decide session.

🚀
Five minutes is a complete experiment
Pick one low-energy task on your list. Open Claude Voice or ChatGPT Advanced Voice. Talk for five minutes. Voice routing turns "can't" into "started" without spending the typing budget you don't have today.
