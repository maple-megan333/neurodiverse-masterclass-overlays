🌅 Morning Brain Dump
📘
What you'll build
Get everything out of your head into a system.
✏️
Try this when you're ready
Paste into Claude: 
My brain is full. Here's everything on my mind: [BRAIN DUMP]

Sort into:
1. DO TODAY (max 3)
2. SCHEDULE LATER (with day)
3. DELEGATE/AUTOMATE
4. LET GO

Be ruthless. 3 things max for today.
⭐
What good looks like
Exactly 3 things in DO TODAY — not 4, not "and also." The "LET GO" pile feels uncomfortable in a good way; you recognize things you've been carrying for weeks. Items in DELEGATE name who to hand them to. You exhale a little reading it.
☁️
Low Energy
"Pick the ONE thing to do first today: [list]"
📅 Routine Builder
📘
What you'll build
Build routines that work with your energy.
✏️
Try this when you're ready
Paste into Claude: 
I want a [morning/evening/weekly] routine. My energy: [DESCRIBE]. Include: [LIST]. Build it respecting my energy. Keep each block under 15 minutes.
⭐
What good looks like
Hard tasks land in your high-energy window, not at 4pm crash time. Each block has a clear start signal (after coffee, before phone). There's a stated fallback for low-energy days — not a guilt clause. You can picture yourself doing this tomorrow without negotiation.
☁️
Low Energy
"Give me a 3-step morning routine in 10 minutes."
🤔 Decision Fatigue Reducer
📘
What you'll build
Eliminate daily micro-decisions.
✏️
Try this when you're ready
Paste into Claude: 
I waste energy on these decisions: [LIST]. For each, give me a simple default system that eliminates the decision.
⭐
What good looks like
Each default is one concrete rule, not a vague "develop a system." Examples: "Monday = pasta night." "Black tee + jeans = work uniform." Defaults survive a bad mood — they work even when you're depleted. You'd actually follow them tomorrow.
☁️
Low Energy
"Pick my dinner tonight. I have [ingredients]."
📚 Learning Plans
📘
What you'll build
Turn 'I want to learn X' into a plan.
✏️
Try this when you're ready
Paste into Claude: 
I want to learn [TOPIC]. I have [TIME/WEEK]. Build a 4-week plan: Foundation → Practice → Build → Teach. Sessions under 25 min.
⭐
What good looks like
Week 1 starts with a 25-min action you could do tonight, not a syllabus. "Build" produces one tangible thing — a working draft, a small project, something you can point at. "Teach" names who you'd explain it to (a friend, a blog post, your kid). No 200-page reading lists.
☁️
Low Energy
"What's the fastest way to start with [topic]? One link, one action."
🏡 Household Automation
📘
What you'll build
Reduce the invisible labor.
✏️
Try this when you're ready
Paste into Claude: 
Help me manage [AREA]. Current system: [DESCRIBE]. Build something that reduces decisions, works when I forget, can be delegated, takes under 5 min/week.
⭐
What good looks like
The system runs on a recurring trigger (a day, a shopping app, a calendar nudge) — not memory. There's a "delegation script" you could text to a partner or sitter verbatim. The fallback for when it breaks is one sentence long. Total upkeep stays under 5 min per week, measurable.
☁️
Low Energy
"Make me a grocery list for the week. Quick meals."
