📧 Email Drafting
📘
What you'll build
Reusable email system matched to your voice.
✏️
Got an unsent email staring at you? Try this
Open Claude and paste: 
You are my email ghostwriter. Style: [DESCRIBE]. I need to write about [SITUATION] to [WHO]. Goal: [OUTCOME]. Under [WORD COUNT] words.
⭐
What good looks like
You can copy-paste the draft and send it with at most 2 word changes. The opening line skips "I hope this finds you well." The ask appears in the first or second sentence — not buried in paragraph 3. Word count stays inside your limit.

📋 Meeting Prep
📘
What you'll build
Never walk in unprepared — AI builds your prep in 2 min.
✏️
Pull up your next meeting and run this
Drop this into Claude: 
Meeting about [TOPIC] with [WHO]. Goal: [DECISION/DISCUSSION]. Give me:
1. 3 things I need to know
2. 2 questions to ask
3. The one thing I must mention
⭐
What good looks like
The 3 facts are specific to who's in the room — not "general industry context." The 2 questions move the decision forward instead of stalling it. The one-thing-to-mention is the line you'd kick yourself for forgetting. Fits on a sticky note.
☁️
Low Energy
"Prep me for a meeting about [topic] in 3 bullets."
📊 Status Reports
📘
What you'll build
Scattered notes → clean report.
✏️
Friday afternoon ritual — try this now
Run this prompt: 
Here are my notes from this week: [PASTE]

Turn into: Summary (2 sentences), Completed, In Progress, Blocked, Next Week Priorities. Under 200 words.
⭐
What good looks like
The summary leads with the win or the blocker — whichever your manager would care about most. "Blocked" names what would unblock it (a decision, a person, a budget line). No fluff verbs like "leveraging" or "synergizing." Word count holds under 200.
☁️
Low Energy
"Summarize my week in 3 bullets: [notes]"
🗂️ Project Planning
📘
What you'll build
Big project → manageable phases.
✏️
Pick a project that's overwhelming you — paste this
Copy this into your AI: 
I need to [PROJECT]. I have [TIME] and [CONSTRAINTS]. Break into phases:
1. This week
2. Milestones
3. Dependencies
4. Minimum viable version if I run out of time
⭐
What good looks like
"This week" has 3-5 concrete tasks, not vague themes. Milestones have dates. Dependencies name the actual person or system you'd be waiting on. The MVP version is something you'd ship without apologizing — the smallest thing that still counts as done.
☁️
Low Energy
"Break [project] into 5 steps. What do I do first?"
