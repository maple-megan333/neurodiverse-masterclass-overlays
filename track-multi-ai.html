What this track teaches

🧠
The premise
No single AI wins every task. Each tool has a real specialty — and forcing one to do everything wastes time, energy, and money. This track maps 21 common task types to the best-fit tool, shows the dominant "Triple Stack" practitioner pattern, and pairs each move to an energy state so a low-spoon day still gets useful work done.

The Triple Stack

Most working professionals in 2026 settle into a three-tool rhythm. Each tool handles the slice it actually does best, and you move between them in a predictable order.

01 Research

Perplexity Pro

Cited, current data. 92% factual accuracy with inline source links. Start here when you need facts you can verify, not vibes.
Free 5/day · Pro $20/mo
02 Synthesis

Claude (Sonnet 4.6 / Opus 4.7)

03 Output

ChatGPT (GPT-5.5)

Social posts, email tone, spreadsheets, image generation, presentations. The versatility tool — the one with the widest surface area.

PERPLEXITY → CLAUDE → CHATGPT

Why no single tool wins

Modern AI models cluster around specialties because their training data, system prompts, and reinforcement learning targets are different. Claude is tuned for long-context reasoning and writing style transfer. GPT-5.5 is tuned for breadth and creative output. Perplexity is a search engine wearing an LLM coat. Gemini 2.5 Pro chases the longest-context PDF analysis crown.

Picking one favorite forces you to pay a tax every time the task doesn't match the tool's grain. A common pattern: someone fights Claude for an hour to make a marketing image (it can't, it's a writing tool), or fights ChatGPT to read a 300-page research corpus (it summarizes badly past ~50k tokens). The cost is real — both in time and in the mental energy spent wrestling.

💡
The shortcut
Match tool to task, not task to tool. If you find yourself fighting a tool for more than 10 minutes, the answer is usually a different tool, not a better prompt.

Energy-state routing

For ND professionals, capacity isn't constant. The right tool at 8am on a high-focus morning is rarely the right tool at 4pm after three meetings. This routing maps your state to your interface.

High energy
Text-based, feature-rich tools. Claude Projects, Cursor 3 for code, n8n for automation builds. You have working memory to spare — spend it on tools that reward setup with depth.
Medium energy
Familiar surfaces, lower setup. ChatGPT for general work, Perplexity for guided search where the task is "find me this and cite it." You want utility without ceremony.
Low energy
Voice mode or audio. Claude Voice (beta, free on all plans) or ChatGPT Advanced Voice when typing is the blocker. NotebookLM Audio Overview if you'd rather consume a research synthesis as a 12-minute podcast than read it.

Three ways to start tomorrow

🎉
Pick one — that's enough

1. Run one Triple Stack pass on a real task. Take something already on your plate — a memo, a research summary, a pitch deck — and walk it through Perplexity → Claude → ChatGPT once. Notice where each tool earned its keep.

2. Set a "voice-only" hour. Pick a low-energy window this week. Use ChatGPT Advanced Voice or Claude Voice for everything in that hour. No typing. See what gets through.

3. Audit your default. If you reach for one AI tool by reflex, write down the last five tasks you used it for. Cross-check against the matrix on the next page. Anything in the wrong column?
