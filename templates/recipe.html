What This Is

Meal planning is often overwhelming: deciding what to cook, checking if you have ingredients, adapting recipes to dietary needs, and planning a week of meals. This is especially challenging for neurodiverse brains that struggle with executive function or decision fatigue.

The AI Recipe Planner uses AI to reduce cognitive load. Instead of you doing all the thinking, AI helps with decisions, adaptations, shopping lists, and weekly planning.

This template gives you a complete workflow for using Claude to make meal planning effortless.

Here's how to use AI to plan your meals from idea to groceries:

The 5-Meal Formula

Can't decide what to cook? Use this framework to limit decision overload:

Plan Around 5 Core Meals

Instead of planning 21 different meals (one per week), pick 5 meals you enjoy. Rotate them, vary sides, change spice levels. This reduces decision fatigue dramatically.

1. Your Favorite Protein + Carb Combo

Chicken + rice, pasta with sauce, fish + potatoes, etc. Something reliable you enjoy.

2. A Quick Meal for Busy Days

Tacos, stir-fry, scrambled eggs + toast. Something you can make in 15-20 minutes.

3. A Comfort Meal

Something that feels easy and safe. Grilled cheese, simple pasta, sandwiches, soup.

4. A Veggie-Forward Meal

A meal that makes you feel good about vegetables. Stir-fry, salad, Buddha bowl, roasted veggies.

5. Something New to Try

One new recipe per week. Keeps things interesting without overwhelming change.

How to Use the Formula:
  • Pick your 5 meals based on what YOU actually like (not what you think you should eat)
  • Plan a rotation: Mon meal 1, Tues meal 2, Wed meal 1 (variation), etc.
  • Swap ingredients between meals (same rice, different proteins)
  • Use AI to vary the meals without starting from scratch

Quick Start Prompts

Prompt 1: Help Me Find 5 Core Meals

Use this when you want to identify your personal 5-meal rotation:
I want to simplify meal planning using 5 core meals I rotate. Tell me about foods I love: [describe foods you enjoy] Foods I dislike: [describe foods to avoid] Dietary restrictions: [any allergies, preferences] Cooking skill level: [beginner/intermediate/advanced] Time I have: [how much time for cooking] Based on this, suggest 5 meals I could rotate throughout the week - one for each category: 1. A favorite protein + carb combo 2. A quick meal for busy days 3. A comfort meal 4. A veggie-forward meal 5. Something new to try For each meal, give me the name, basic ingredients, and why it fits that category.

Prompt 2: Adapt a Recipe to My Needs

Use this when you have a recipe but want to modify it:
[Paste the recipe here] Please adapt this recipe for me: - Servings: [number you need] - Dietary needs: [allergies, preferences] - Substitutions: [ingredients you want to swap] - Cooking style: [faster, easier, simpler, fewer dishes, etc.] - Dietary focus: [lower sodium, more veggies, less sugar, etc.] Give me the adapted recipe with ingredient list, step-by-step instructions, and notes on what changed.

Prompt 3: Plan a Week of Meals

Use this to create a complete week's meal plan:
Create a 7-day meal plan for me with these parameters: My 5 core meals are: [List your 5 meals] Number of people: [solo, couple, family with kids] Cooking time available: [weekday hours, weekend hours] Ingredients I have on hand: [list what's in your pantry/fridge] Ingredients to use up: [anything expiring soon] Dietary needs: [allergies, preferences] For each day, show: - Breakfast - Lunch - Dinner - One snack idea Keep variety by rotating meals and changing sides/flavors. Then give me a consolidated shopping list organized by category.

Prompt 4: Generate a Smart Shopping List

Use this to create organized shopping lists from your meal plan:
I have this meal plan for the week: [Paste your 7-day meal plan] Create a shopping list organized by: - Produce (with quantities in weights/units) - Meat & Seafood - Dairy & Eggs - Pantry (staples, seasonings) - Frozen items For each item, include: - Exact quantity needed - Any quality notes (organic, specific type, etc.) - Storage tips - Substitutions if not available Organize it so I can take it to the store (or order online) efficiently. Note any items I can prep ahead.

Prompt 5: Plan from a URL Recipe

Use this when you have a recipe URL you want to adapt and plan around:
I found this recipe: [Paste the URL or describe where it's from] Please: 1. Summarize what the recipe makes 2. Adapt it for [your dietary needs/time/skill level] 3. Suggest 4 other meals to pair it with for a week-long plan 4. Create a shopping list for all 5 meals Make sure the adapted version is easier/faster/simpler than the original while keeping the essential flavors.

Why This Works for Neurodiverse Brains

🎯 Reduces Decision Fatigue

Instead of deciding what to cook every day, you rotate 5 familiar meals. AI handles the variations so it stays interesting without being overwhelming.

📋 Removes Planning Burden

AI creates the meal plan, shopping list, and organization. You get a clear checklist to execute, not abstract decisions.

⚙️ Handles Complexity

Multiple dietary needs, ingredient substitutions, quantities, storage. AI manages all this so you don't have to.

🔄 Creates Routine

Rotating the same 5 meals creates predictable routine, which many neurodiverse people find calming and energizing.

✅ Ensures Follow-Through

Clear shopping lists and meal plans make it easier to actually cook, not just plan to cook.

💡 Preserves Mental Energy

By automating planning and decisions, you preserve energy for the actual cooking and eating parts you care about.

Advanced Tips

Use AI for Real-Time Help

Don't just use AI at the planning stage. Use it while cooking:

  • Stuck on a step? "I don't understand step 4, help me simplify it"
  • Ingredient problem? "I don't have X, what can I substitute?"
  • Time running short? "How can I finish this meal in 10 minutes?"
  • Need encouragement? "I'm overwhelmed cooking. Give me one simple thing to do next."

Build a Recipe Library

Save conversations with adapted recipes. Over time, build a personal recipe collection tailored to your needs, preferences, and cooking style.

This becomes your custom cookbook that AI can reference for future planning.

Adapt Based on Energy Levels

Neurodiverse brains have variable energy. Tell AI about your energy that day:

  • "I have low energy today, what's the quickest meal from my plan?"
  • "I'm having a good day, can I prep anything for later in the week?"
  • "I'm overwhelmed, what's the absolute simplest thing I can make?"

Batch Prep with AI Help

Ask AI what you can prep on weekends to make weekday cooking easier:

"Based on my meal plan, what can I prep on Sunday to make weekday cooking faster? Give me a 2-hour prep timeline."
