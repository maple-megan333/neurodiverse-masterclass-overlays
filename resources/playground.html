🧪 Fun Experiments

Try these creative experiments to explore what AI can do. Perfect for learning and having fun!

🎭 AI Debate Tournament

Have Claude argue different perspectives on a topic, then switch sides and debate itself!

Round 1: "You are a constitutional scholar arguing that AI regulation should be strict. Make your strongest argument in 3 paragraphs." Round 2: "Now argue the opposite position - that AI regulation should be minimal. Make your strongest argument against your previous position." Round 3: "Now you're a judge evaluating both arguments. Which side made the more compelling case and why?"
Tip: Try this with different topics like remote work, climate policy, or education reform. You'll get nuanced perspectives by forcing the AI to steelman both sides.

🔍 Reverse Interview

Instead of you interviewing Claude, have Claude interview you to learn about your unique perspective.

You are a curious interviewer. Ask me 5 thoughtful questions designed to understand: 1. How I think differently than most people 2. What problems I see that others miss 3. What I'm passionate about that most people don't care about 4. My unique perspective on the future Make the questions conversational and interesting, not like a typical interview.
Tip: This is great for self-discovery and getting personalized insights. Claude will ask follow-ups that help you articulate things you've never put into words.

🌍 World Building

Create an entire fictional world with consistent rules, history, and characters.

Help me build a fantasy world with these elements: - A unique magic system (not wand-based) - A geography that determines the culture - A history of three major conflicts - Four distinct cultures with different values - A problem that requires characters from different cultures to work together For each element, give me 2-3 detailed options to choose from.
Tip: Iteratively refine each element. Ask Claude to explain how cultures would interact, what conflicts would naturally arise, and how magic would shape society.

💡 Innovation Workshop

Use Claude to brainstorm solutions to real problems using structured thinking techniques.

I'm trying to solve: [Your specific problem] Let's use the SCAMPER method: - Substitute: What if we replaced X with Y? - Combine: What if we merged this with that? - Adapt: What exists elsewhere that we could adapt? - Modify: What if we changed the size, shape, or attributes? - Put to another use: What else could this be used for? - Eliminate: What if we removed this element? - Reverse: What if we did the opposite? Give me creative ideas for each category.
Tip: Pick the most interesting ideas and ask Claude to elaborate, combine, or refine them. You might discover solutions you never would have thought of.

📚 Create Your Own Skill

Develop a specialized AI persona that becomes an expert in your domain.

You are now an expert [your domain] coach. Your role is to: 1. Understand how people think differently in this field 2. Identify where they're getting stuck 3. Teach them the mental models that experts use 4. Challenge assumptions that hold them back Introduce yourself and ask me about my background in [domain]. Then, guide me toward deeper expertise using Socratic questioning before giving direct advice.
Tip: After using this once, save the system prompt you create. You can refine it over time to build increasingly sophisticated expert personae tailored to how you like to learn.

🎯 Tips for Better AI Experiments

  • Be specific: The more context you give, the better the results
  • Iterate: Refine prompts based on what works. AI is a conversation, not a search engine
  • Experiment with tone: Try different personas and perspectives to find what resonates
  • Use structure: Lists, numbered steps, and frameworks help AI think clearly
  • Save what works: Keep prompts that generate valuable results for reuse
  • Go deeper: Ask follow-up questions. The second and third answers are often the best
  • Mix tools: Generate images with Midjourney, make audio with ElevenLabs, build with code from Claude
